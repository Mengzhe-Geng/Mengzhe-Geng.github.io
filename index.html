My name is GENG Mengzhe /kɤ̌ŋ mʊŋˋ ʈʂɛˊ/, and I am currently a PhD student in Cognitive Science and Language at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona.

I work on syntax, semantics and their interface, with a particular focus on the theories of verb meaning and motion verbs. I have done research in both theoretical linguistics and psycholinguistics, including studies on Mandarin Chinese and Shanghai Sign Language (the Shanghai variety of Chinese Sign Language). I also speak Spanish and would be glad to conduct comparative studies among Mandarin, Shanghai Sign Language, English, Spanish, and even other languages.

In my PhD program, I aim to explore how the three intertwined aspects, i.e., word category, manner and result entailments, and telicity realization, interact within event structure and influence the basic skeleton of a predicate.
